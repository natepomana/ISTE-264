HW1 - Introduction of Loader.js

Name pending.


Goals of the project / What's the problem?

Loading times can vary depending on different variables such as device, internet connection, and location. Most sites don't have some form of loading screen to show that the site is actually loading. Instead, users may be staring at a blank screen or a partially loaded webpage. This is a problem because users may leave the page, or spend time trying to debug the problem, when in reality their site is loading and it's just taking some amount of time. If a loading screen, such as a wheel, was shown on a webpage, this could prevent users from deviating from the site and spengind time debugging a problem that is not necessarially a problem.

Target Analysis

Potentially anyone could use this product, but specifically, those who are not patient.

Scope

This should NOT be used as a solution to all loading problems. The intent is to give a visual for users to look at as a website is loading. If a user is looking at motion, there is a chance they may stay. I don't have any proof of this which is what I want to examine also with this.

The Problem

People are impatient. We go on websites that show some sort of "loading" mechanism - just a blank white screen. What if there was a way for developers, and potentially users, to use and see a loading animation? Enter Loader.js

The Plan

View other "loader" options, whether frameworks, Chrome Extensions, or implementations of them.
Do comparison with sample users of two sites - one that has no loading animation, and the other with a loader animation. Compare reactions/wait time/patience of users.
Based on that, create sample loaders for developers to use. The sample loaders could be easily copied and pasted into developer's code and would work.
